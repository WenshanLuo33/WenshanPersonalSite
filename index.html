I am a student in the MSAUD program at Cornell University. I completed my five-year undergraduate architecture program at Wuhan University in China. I have a strong interest in architecture, urban design, and theory, and I am currently exploring my own style and research methodology.

My Projects

A home designed for mobile workers for a while.

As Yangzhou expands, demolition creates recyclable bricks. Brickmen collect and repurpose them, aided by a mobile, balloon-based structure. They live, work, and sort bricks for sale or reuse, moving to the next site once finished. The design is flexible, sustainable, and innovative.

Historic building renovation project - the yardstick of time

Dazhimen railway station in Wuhan’s Hankou district, once abandoned, is now an urban oasis where nature intertwines with history. Vines and greenery reclaim the ruins, creating a balance between past and present. This transformation highlights the city’s evolving relationship with nature amid urban growth.

Hospital and nursing home complex for the elderly

The Empathic Hospital Project combines a hospital and nursing home to connect generations. It fosters empathy by helping young people experience aging challenges and interact with seniors. Shared spaces and activities bring vitality to elderly lives while promoting understanding and care.

Enclave and studentification Study in University of Glasgow

The project in Glasgow’s Yorkhill area examines student enclaves and their impact on local communities. It proposes a network of public spaces, improved streets, and green corridors to enhance student-local interactions. Centralizing student housing aims to create a more inclusive and sustainable community.
